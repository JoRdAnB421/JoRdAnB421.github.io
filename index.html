I'm a data scientist with a PhD in computational astrophysics and gravitational waves from Cardiff University. My research involved digging into LIGO data — the kind of signals that tell us two black holes just collided a billion light-years away.

These days I work in data science, but the curiosity that drove the PhD never really switched off. I make indie games in my spare time — mostly small, atmospheric things built in Godot — and I do public science talks whenever I get the chance, including at Pints of Knowledge events in London.

I'm also building a YouTube channel on astrophysics: plain-English explainers on black holes, dark matter, gravitational waves, and whatever else I'm currently obsessing over. And occasionally I stream dev work on Twitch because it turns out having an audience makes you actually finish things.
